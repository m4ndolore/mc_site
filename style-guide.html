Design Philosophy

Merge Combinator operates across two interface tiers: public marketing and authenticated C2UX operational consoles. The transition between them should feel natural and seamless.

Marketing Tier

Aspirational & Accessible

Public-facing pages emphasize mission and vision. Gradient backgrounds, animated visuals, and bento grid layouts communicate innovation and credibility. Typography is larger, spacing is generous. The goal is to inspire and convert.

C2UX Operational Tier

Dense & Functional

Authenticated interfaces prioritize information density and task completion. Panel-based layouts, monospace data displays, and status indicators create a command-center aesthetic. Sharp edges (2px max) and minimal shadows reinforce operational credibility.

Design Principles

Core tenets guiding all interface decisions.

01
Operational Credibility

Every interface element should reinforce that this is a serious platform for national security work. Sharp edges, dense information, and operational language build trust.

02
Signal Over Noise

Subdued backgrounds let status colors and data stand out. Blue is the primary signal color. Green, yellow, and red are reserved for status.

03
Seamless Transition

The journey from marketing to operational interface should feel like one cohesive experience, not two different products. Shared navigation, typography, and color language create continuity.

04
Dense by Default

C2UX interfaces prioritize information density. Users should see more data with less scrolling. Marketing pages can breathe; operational pages should work.

05
Monospace for Data

Timestamps, counts, codes, and labels use monospace typography. This creates a terminal-like credibility and improves scanability of numerical information.

06
No Decorative Excess

Shadows are minimal. Gradients are reserved for marketing heroes. Animations serve function, not decoration. Every visual element must earn its place.
