Desert Rotor

MIRA X & COMMAND XD
Alumni Command and Control Multi Both TRL 9 10-20 people

Overview

Problem Unmanned operations are fragmented. Air, land, and maritime platform often requires its own ground control system (GCS), creating training burdens, poor interoperability, and mission delays. In contested or denied environments, fragile communications and non-resilient networks increase operational risk.

Solution Desert Rotor provides a single, interoperable GCS for all unmanned assets with redundant mesh networking and integrated 5G/SATCOM for resilient beyond-line-of-sight control. Currently advancing full multi-domain operations (MDO) capability.

Field Validation 550+ GCS deployed globally since 2020 with U.S. and allied defense and commercial operators. Proven in rugged environments with near‑zero downtime.

Technology Maturity TRL 9 - Operationally proven hardware/software in production and fielded worldwide. Next-gen hardware in final pre-production.

Strategic Advantage Proprietary cross‑platform HID/GCS stack independent of Windows/Linux/Android, patented integration workflows, unmatched interoperability, and 5+ years of deployment know‑how.

Go‑to‑Market Direct DoD access (Army, Navy, DLA), contract vehicle companies, D2P2 SBIR awardee, expanding via SWMAC, client resellers

Dual‑Use Energy, mining, environmental monitoring, disaster response, heavy‑lift, high‑altitude any robust unmanned control requirement.

Team Debin Ray (CEO), Aaron Frank (CFO), Joe Burch (Principal Engineer), James Roe (VP Defense), Marc Chesley (Software Advisor).

Competitive Landscape Competing with Tomahawk Robotics, UxV Technologies, Parry Labs, Mobilicom. Desert Rotor offers true platform‑agnostic control and MDO‑ready integration others lack.

Primary User Frontline operators: ISR teams, C‑UAS crews, unmanned pilots.

User‑Critical Problem Multiple incompatible controllers and fragile comms slow missions, increase cognitive load, and limit operational flexibility
