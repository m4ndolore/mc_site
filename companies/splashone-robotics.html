SplashOne Robotics

Experimental Fighter Drone - One
Applicant Force Protection (Defense) Air Multi Land Sea TRL 5 Series Funding 3-5 people

Overview

Problem American forces lack a reliable, cost effective way to defeat Group 1–2 UAS threats.

Solution Physics and tactics points towards an inevitable best practice: air-to-air kinetic kills via durable, autonomous, aerodynamically efficient platforms. SplashOne builds fully autonomous, man portable fighter drones that detect and destroy hostile aircraft with high reliability, at long ranges, and with reusable platforms that return between missions for more ammunition and fresh batteries before taking off to do it again.

Field Validation/TRL TRL-5. Subsystems have been prototyped and bench-tested; airframe has been flight-tested with integrated airsoft gun pod. Targeting and detection modules have been demonstrated against simulated threats in controlled environments.

Strategic Advantage Compared with competing approaches: Massive cost advantage per kill; dramatically longer range, functions in comms-out environments, reliable takedowns, re-usable, autonomous --> low workload for warfighter.

Go-to-Market Access Founding team has unfair advantage: recently departed active duty where they were the experts consulted by decision makers, or themselves making decisions on these systems. The people with checkbooks are their personal friends and comrades in arms going back decades.

Dual-Use Potential Commercial security (oil & gas, maritime, critical infrastructure), border surveillance, and VIP venue defense. Air-to-ground applications for agriculture, public safety.

Team SOF Veterans and spouses. A-List experience: MIT, Harvard, Boeing, Apple, USSOCOM, AFRL.

Competitive Landscape None, outside of Ukraine. Closest thing western gov'ts have are bulky multi-copters with minimal autonomy and negligible range.

Primary User Warfighters at the edge who need to deny use of the airspace to adversaries.
