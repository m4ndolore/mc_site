Overview

BKPK delivers immediate, clean, portable, and dependable energy anywhere, anytime, and at any scale. As tactical environments shift toward more dispersed and decentralized camps, warfighters and responders face growing risks to safety, readiness, and continuity from traditional fuel-based power systems. BKPK answers with rugged, single-person-portable battery systems that provide power when and where it’s needed — no noise, no fuel, no delay. Built to withstand harsh handling and extreme field conditions, each unit is drop-proof, waterproof (including its connectors), and engineered for reliable performance in any environment. The system is designed to be stupid-proof in the field—intuitive, tool-free, and resistant to common operator error, even under pressure. The modular packs work alone or in swarms, with composability from one to thousands, drawing from any source and adapting to mission needs with built-in edge intelligence.

Currently at TRL 5, BKPK’s Gen 5 units are undergoing lab testing and will enter field trials in Q3 2025 through a DLX-led zero-fuel camp pilot. Additional trials with military and emergency response teams are in planning. With over $45M in commercial MOUs and active partner pathways in solar, drone, and defense logistics, BKPK is scaling fast. Backed by 30+ IP vectors and a veteran team from NASA, Accenture, Daimler, Motorola, Flex, and Jabil, BKPK is not a science project — it’s a mission-ready energy platform built for defense, response, and the future of distributed power.
